ln(), lg() and log(): do they make any difference in Machine Learning?

Normally we head that $ln$ is used in physics, $log_2$ is used in computer science and $log_{10}$ is used in mathematics. Here we will prove that the difference of the base can be represented with timing a constant.

Proof. $$ \frac{log_a(x)}{log_b(x)} = constant$$

Here $a > 0$, $b > 0$, $x > 0$

Suppose $ x = (ab)^t $, $t > 0$

$$ \frac{log_a(x)}{log_b(x)} = \frac{log_a((ab)^t)}{log_b((ab)^t)} = \frac{t (1 + log_ab)}{t (1 + log_ba)} = \frac{1 + log_ab}{1 + \frac{1}{log_ab}} = log_ab$$

End.
